Building a Semantic Search Engine using an Ontology: A Case Study on Social Media Influencers.

Jan 2023 - Current

Undergraduate Thesis
In our work we explored the creation of a more effective method for identifying relevant social media influencers through an ontology-based semantic search engine. Traditional keyword-based search methods often fail to identify the best influencers, so this study aims to improve the process by defining concepts, relationships, and metrics that categorize influencers based on audience location, interaction rates, and content types. We assumed the user has a primary idea of the ontology. In our current work, we aim to address the assumptions made in our previous research by fine-tuning LLaMA2 and employing prompt engineering techniques. This approach will allow us to perform a comparative analysis between the results obtained from LLaMA2 and those from our earlier methodology. Through this comparison, we seek to assess the improvements in performance and effectiveness of these advanced techniques over the original framework.

Field: Natural Language, Semantic Search
